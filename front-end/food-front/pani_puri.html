Puchka (Pani Puri)

Puchka, also known as Pani Puri, is a popular Indian street food consisting of hollow, crispy puris filled with spicy tamarind water, chickpeas, and potatoes.

Time: 30 minutes

Protein:

  • Chickpeas (1 cup) provide around 15g of protein.
  • Potatoes (if used) add minimal protein but contribute to the flavor.
  • Semolina (1 cup) adds about 6g of protein.
  • Coriander and mint add negligible protein but are rich in vitamins.
  • Chaat masala may contain small amounts of protein depending on ingredients.

Ingredients

Steps to Make Puchka:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. Prepare the Puri:
    • In a bowl, combine 1 cup of semolina (sooji) and 2 tablespoons of all-purpose flour (maida). Add a pinch of salt.
    • Gradually add water to form a smooth, stiff dough. Let it rest for about 30 minutes.
    • Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
    • Take small portions of the dough and roll them into small balls. Roll each ball into thin circles.
    • Fry the puris in hot oil until they puff up and turn golden brown. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  2. Prepare the Filling:
    • Boil 1 cup of chickpeas and 2-3 medium-sized potatoes until soft.
    • Peel and mash the potatoes, then mix with the boiled chickpeas.
    • Add finely chopped onions, green chilies, and fresh coriander leaves.
    • Season with salt, chaat masala, and lemon juice to taste.
  3. Prepare the Pani (Spicy Water):
    • In a blender, combine 1 cup of fresh mint leaves, 1/2 cup of coriander leaves, 2-3 green chilies, a small piece of ginger, and 1/4 cup of tamarind pulp.
    • Add water, salt, and a pinch of roasted cumin powder. Blend until smooth.
    • Adjust the seasoning and dilute with water to your desired taste and spiciness.
  4. Assemble the Puchka:
    • Gently crack the top of each puri to create an opening.
    • Stuff each puri with the filling mixture.
    • Dip the filled puri into the spicy pani or serve it on the side for dipping.
  5. Serve Immediately:
    • Puchkas are best enjoyed immediately after assembling to maintain the crispiness of the puris.

Variations:

  • Fruit Pani Puri: Add fruits like pomegranate or mango to the filling for a sweet twist.
  • Spicy Pani: Adjust the chili and spices in the pani for extra heat.
