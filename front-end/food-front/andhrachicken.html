Andhra Chicken

Andhra Chicken is a spicy and flavorful dish from the Andhra Pradesh region of India, made with marinated chicken cooked in a rich blend of spices, onions, and tomatoes. Perfectly paired with rice or roti!

Time: 1 hour

Protein:

  • Chicken (500g) provides approximately 100g of protein.

Recipe Details

Ingredients

Steps to Make Andhra Chicken:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice. Mix well to coat the chicken evenly. Cover and let it marinate for at least 1 hour (or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor).
  2. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add the slit green chilies and curry leaves; sauté for another minute.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and start to break down.
  5. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for a few more minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes until the oil starts to separate.
  7. Add the marinated chicken to the pan. Stir well to combine with the spice mixture.
  8. Cover and cook on medium heat for about 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  9. Once the chicken is cooked, check the seasoning and adjust if necessary. Add garam masala and stir well.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a splash of lemon juice before serving.
  11. Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.
