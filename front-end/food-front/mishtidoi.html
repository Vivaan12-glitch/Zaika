Mishti Doi

Mishti Doi is a traditional Bengali dessert made from sweetened yogurt, often flavored with cardamom and served in clay pots.

Time: 12 hours (including setting time)

Protein:

  • Yogurt (1 cup) provides around 8g of protein.

Recipe Details

Ingredients

Steps to Make Mishti Doi:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring 1 liter of full-fat milk to a boil. Stir occasionally to prevent it from sticking to the bottom.
  2. Once it reaches a boil, reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring frequently until it thickens slightly.
  3. Stir in 1/2 cup of sugar and 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder into the hot milk. Mix well until the sugar dissolves completely.
  4. Remove the pan from heat and let the milk mixture cool down to a warm temperature (around 40-45°C or 104-113°F).
  5. In a small bowl, mix 2 tablespoons of plain, unsweetened yogurt with a little bit of the warm milk to temper it. Then, pour this mixture back into the rest of the warm milk. Stir gently to combine.
  6. Pour the mixture into clean clay pots or a glass container. Cover it with a lid or a cloth to keep it warm. Let it sit undisturbed in a warm place for about 6-8 hours, or until it sets and becomes thick.
  7. Once set, refrigerate the Misti Doi for a few hours to cool completely.
  8. Before serving, you can garnish with chopped nuts (like almonds or pistachios) and a few strands of saffron if desired.
  9. Serve the Misti Doi chilled as a delightful dessert. Enjoy your sweet and creamy Misti Doi!
