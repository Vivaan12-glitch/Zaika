Malpua

Malpua is a traditional Indian sweet made from a batter of flour, sugar, and ripe bananas, deep-fried to golden perfection and often served with rabri (sweetened condensed milk).

Time: 45 minutes

Protein:

  • Flour (1 cup) provides approximately 10g of protein.
  • Milk (1 cup) adds about 8g of protein.

Recipe Details

Ingredients

Steps to Make Malpua:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of all-purpose flour (maida) and 1/2 cup of semolina (rava).
  2. Add 3/4 cup of sugar, 1 ripe mashed banana, and 1 cup of milk (adjust as needed) to the dry ingredients. Mix well until you have a smooth batter, adjusting with water if necessary.
  3. Stir in 1/2 teaspoon of cardamom powder, 1/2 teaspoon of fennel seeds (optional), and 1/2 teaspoon of baking powder.
  4. Let the batter rest for about 15-30 minutes to soften the mixture.
  5. In a deep pan or kadhai, heat ghee or oil for frying. Ensure the oil is hot enough but not smoking.
  6. Once the oil is hot, pour a ladleful of batter into the oil, spreading it slightly to form a round shape. Fry until the edges turn golden brown, then flip and cook the other side until golden.
  7. Remove the fried Malpua from the oil and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  8. If desired, prepare sugar syrup by boiling 1 cup of sugar with 1 cup of water until it reaches a one-string consistency. Add crushed cardamom pods and saffron for flavor.
  9. If using sugar syrup, dip the hot Malpua in the syrup for a few seconds to soak.
  10. Garnish with chopped nuts (cashews, almonds) and grated coconut if desired.
  11. Serve warm or at room temperature as a delicious dessert or snack.
