Arisapitha

Arisapitha is a traditional Odia sweet dish made from rice flour, filled with a mixture of grated coconut and jaggery, and then steamed. It's a festive delicacy often prepared during festivals.

Time: 1 hour

Protein:

  • Coconut (1 cup) provides around 3g of protein.

Ingredients

Steps to Make Arisapitha:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. In a bowl, combine the rice flour, grated jaggery, coconut, cardamom powder, and salt (if using).
  2. Gradually add water to the mixture and knead it into a soft, pliable dough. The consistency should be similar to that of chapati dough.
  3. Take a small portion of the dough and flatten it into a round disc (about 4-5 inches in diameter) using your palms. If using sesame seeds, sprinkle some on top of the disc and press gently.
  4. Heat ghee in a pan or tawa over medium heat.
  5. Once the ghee is hot, place the shaped disc onto the pan. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until it turns golden brown and crispy.
  6. Remove the cooked Arisa Pithas from the pan and place them on a paper towel to absorb excess ghee.
  7. Serve warm as a snack or dessert. They can also be enjoyed with tea or coffee.
