Sarso da Saag with Makki di Roti

A traditional Punjabi dish made from mustard greens and served with corn flour flatbread, perfect for winter meals.

Time: 40 minutes

Protein:

  • Sarso da Saag provides approximately 3-5g of protein per serving (1 cup).
  • Makki di Roti adds around 2-3g of protein per roti.
  • Total Combined Protein: Approximately 5-8g per serving.

Recipe Details

Ingredients

Steps to Make Sarso da Saag:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. Wash and chop 2 cups of mustard greens (sarson) and 1 cup of spinach (optional).
  2. In a large pot, add the chopped greens, 4 cups of water, and a pinch of salt. Cook over medium heat until the greens are tender (about 20-25 minutes).
  3. Once cooked, drain excess water if necessary and blend the greens to a smooth paste. Return the blended mixture to the pot.
  4. Add 2 tablespoons of butter or ghee, 1-2 tablespoons of maize flour (makki ka atta), and spices (like red chili powder, turmeric, and salt) to taste. Cook for another 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Steps to Make Makki di Roti:

  1. In a bowl, take 2 cups of maize flour (makki ka atta). Gradually add warm water and knead to form a smooth dough.
  2. Divide the dough into small balls. Flatten each ball into a round shape using your hands or a rolling pin, dusting with extra flour to prevent sticking.
  3. Heat a griddle (tawa) over medium heat. Place the roti on the griddle and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side until golden brown. Optionally, brush with ghee while cooking.

Serving:

Serve the hot Sarso da Saag topped with a dollop of ghee alongside Makki di Roti. Enjoy with a side of pickles and yogurt for a complete meal!

Variations:

  • Spicy Saag: Add chopped green chilies for extra heat.
  • Cheesy Saag: Stir in some grated cheese for a creamy texture.
