Rasabali

Rasabali is a traditional sweet dish from Kendrapara, Odisha. It consists of deep-fried chhena patties soaked in thickened, sweetened milk infused with cardamom flavor.

Time: 1 hour

Protein:

  • Chhena (cottage cheese) provides around 18g of protein per 100g.
  • Milk adds 8g of protein per cup.

Ingredients

Steps to Make Rasabali:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. In a large bowl, combine fresh chhena with semolina, sugar, cardamom powder, and a pinch of baking powder. Knead well until smooth.
  2. Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into flat, round patties without cracks.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan. Fry the chhena patties until golden brown on all sides. Remove and place them on a paper towel to drain excess oil.
  4. In another pan, bring milk to a boil and simmer until it reduces to half its volume. Add sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using).
  5. Gently add the fried chhena patties into the thickened milk and let them soak for about 15-20 minutes.
  6. Garnish with chopped dry fruits like almonds, cashews, and pistachios, if desired.
  7. Serve the Rasabali warm or chilled as per your preference.
