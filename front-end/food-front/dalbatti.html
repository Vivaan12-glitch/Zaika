Dal Batti Churma

Dal Batti Churma is a traditional Rajasthani dish consisting of lentil curry (dal), baked wheat balls (batti), and a sweet mixture of crushed wheat and jaggery (churma).

Time: 1.5 hours

Protein:

  • Lentils (1 cup) provide about 18g of protein.
  • Whole wheat flour (1 cup) adds approximately 10g of protein.

Steps to Make Dal Batti Churma:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. Cook the Dal: Rinse the dals thoroughly and soak them for about 30 minutes. Pressure cook the dals with 3 cups of water until soft (about 3-4 whistles).
  2. Make the Tempering: In a pan, heat ghee. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add Spices: Stir in ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add Tomatoes: Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  5. Combine with Dal: Add the cooked dal, salt, and water as needed to adjust the consistency. Simmer for 10-15 minutes. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  6. Preparing the Batti: Make the Dough: In a bowl, mix whole wheat flour, semolina, baking powder, and salt. Add melted ghee and mix well. Gradually add water to form a stiff dough.
  7. Shape the Batti: Divide the dough into small balls and flatten them slightly.
  8. Cook the Batti: Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Place the batti on a baking tray and bake for about 20-25 minutes, turning them halfway until golden brown. Alternatively, you can also cook them on a tandoor or an open flame until they are charred.
  9. Preparing the Churma: Make the Churma: Roast the whole wheat flour in ghee until it turns golden brown. Let it cool.
  10. Mix with Jaggery: Once cooled, mix the roasted flour with grated jaggery and cardamom powder. Add chopped nuts if desired.
  11. Serving: Serve the dal hot with batti drizzled with ghee, and serve the churma on the side, allowing everyone to mix it with the dal and batti as they like.
