Idli

Idli is a traditional South Indian steamed rice cake, often served with sambar and coconut chutney. It's light, fluffy, and a favorite breakfast option.

Time: 30 minutes (plus soaking time)

Protein:

  • Rice (1 cup) provides around 4g of protein.
  • Urad dal (1/2 cup) adds about 13g of protein.

Recipe Details

Ingredients

Steps to Make Idli:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. Soak 1 cup of idli rice and 1 cup of urad dal (split black gram) separately in water for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and dal. Blend the urad dal with a little water until smooth, then add the soaked rice and blend until slightly coarse. Add more water as needed.
  3. Transfer the batter to a large bowl, cover, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours until it doubles in volume.
  4. Grease the idli molds with oil or ghee to prevent sticking. Fill the molds with the fermented batter about three-quarters full.
  5. Place the filled molds in a steamer and steam for about 10-15 minutes or until a toothpick inserted comes out clean.
  6. Remove the idlis from the molds and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
