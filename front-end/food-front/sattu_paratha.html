Sattu Paratha

Sattu Paratha is a wholesome Indian flatbread made with roasted gram flour, spices, and herbs, offering a nutritious and flavorful meal.

Time: 30 minutes

Protein:

  • Sattu (1 cup) provides around 26g of protein.
  • Whole wheat flour (2 cups) adds about 14g of protein.

Steps to Make Sattu Paratha:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. In a large bowl, mix whole wheat flour and salt, then gradually add water to knead into a smooth, soft dough. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  2. In another bowl, combine the sattu flour with chopped onion, green chilies, coriander leaves, ginger, lemon juice, salt, carom seeds, and black pepper. Mix well, adding a little water if necessary to form a thick paste.
  3. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls (about the size of a golf ball) and flatten each slightly, dusting with flour.
  4. Roll out each ball into a small circle (about 4-5 inches in diameter), place a spoonful of the sattu filling in the center, and bring the edges together to seal.
  5. Gently flatten the stuffed dough ball and roll it out into a larger circle (about 7-8 inches).
  6. Heat a tava (griddle) or non-stick pan over medium heat. Cook each paratha for about 1-2 minutes on each side until golden brown, applying ghee or oil as desired.
  7. Repeat the process for remaining dough and filling. Serve hot with yogurt, pickles, or salad.
