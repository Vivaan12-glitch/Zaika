Dosa

Dosa is a thin, crispy pancake made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It is a popular South Indian dish, usually served with sambar and chutney.

Time: 30 minutes (plus soaking and fermentation)

Protein:

  • Rice (1 cup) provides around 4g of protein.
  • Urad dal (1/2 cup) adds about 13g of protein.

Steps to Make Dosa:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. Soak the Ingredients:
    • Rinse the urad dal, rice, and fenugreek seeds (if using) separately.
    • Soak them in water for at least 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Grind the Batter:
    • Drain the soaked ingredients.
    • In a blender or wet grinder, combine the urad dal and fenugreek seeds with a little water. Blend until smooth and creamy.
    • Add the rice and blend until you achieve a slightly coarse texture (you can add more water as needed).
    • Transfer the batter to a large bowl, cover, and let it ferment for 8-12 hours in a warm place until it doubles in volume.
  3. Prepare the Dosa:
    • Once the batter is fermented, gently stir it and add salt to taste.
    • Heat a non-stick or cast-iron skillet on medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  4. Cook the Dosa:
    • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the skillet and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin layer.
    • Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges and on top of the dosa.
    • Cook until the edges start to lift and the bottom turns golden brown (about 2-3 minutes).
  5. Flip and Serve:
    • If desired, flip the dosa and cook for an additional 30 seconds.
    • Remove the dosa from the skillet and keep it warm. Repeat with the remaining batter.
    • Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any side dish of your choice.
