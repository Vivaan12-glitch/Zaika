Litti Chokha

Litti Chokha is a traditional dish from Bihar, India, consisting of round wheat balls stuffed with spiced sattu (roasted gram flour) and served with chokha, a spicy mashed mixture of eggplant, tomato, and potatoes.

Time: 1.5 hours

Protein:

  • Sattu (1 cup) provides approximately 25g of protein.

Recipe Details

Ingredients

Steps to Make Litti Chokha:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a mixing bowl, combine 2 cups of whole wheat flour, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 3 tablespoons of ghee. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth and firm dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the Filling: In another bowl, mix 1 cup of sattu, 1 finely chopped onion, 2-3 finely chopped green chilies, 1-inch grated ginger, and 4-5 minced garlic cloves. Add the juice of 1 lemon, a handful of chopped coriander leaves, salt to taste, 1/2 teaspoon of ajwain, and 1 tablespoon of mustard oil (optional). Mix well to combine.
  3. Shape the Litti: Divide the rested dough into equal portions (about 10-12). Flatten each portion and place a tablespoon of the sattu filling in the center. Gather the edges and seal them to form a ball. Gently flatten the ball to form a disc.
  4. Cook the Litti: Preheat an oven or tandoor. If using an oven, set it to 200°C (392°F). Place the littis on a baking tray and bake for 20-25 minutes, turning them occasionally, until they are golden brown and cooked through. If using a tandoor, cook the littis until they are nicely charred and cooked.
  5. Baste with Ghee: Remove the littis and baste them with ghee while they are hot.
  6. For Chokha: Roast 1 large eggplant, 2 medium tomatoes, and 2 boiled potatoes over an open flame or in an oven until charred and cooked through. Once cooled, peel the charred skin off the eggplant and potatoes. In a mixing bowl, mash the roasted eggplant, tomatoes, and boiled potatoes together. Add 4-5 roasted garlic cloves, 1-2 finely chopped green chilies, 1 small finely chopped onion (optional), a handful of chopped coriander leaves, 1 tablespoon of mustard oil, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  7. Serve: Serve the hot littis with the chokha on the side, along with extra ghee for dipping. Enjoy your authentic Litti Chokha!
