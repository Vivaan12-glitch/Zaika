Chitau Pitha

Chitau Pitha is a traditional rice cake from Odisha, typically made with rice flour and filled with a mixture of grated coconut and jaggery, then steamed to perfection. It's a delicious snack often enjoyed during festivals.

Time: 1 hour (plus cooling)

Protein:

  • Coconut (1 cup) provides around 3g of protein.

Ingredients

Steps to Make Chitau Pitha:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. In a bowl, soak 1 cup of rice for 4-5 hours and then grind it into a smooth batter.
  2. In another bowl, mix 1 cup of grated coconut and 1/2 cup of jaggery until well combined.
  3. Heat a steamer and lightly grease the steaming plates.
  4. In a bowl, mix the rice flour and a pinch of salt. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth, pliable dough. The consistency should be similar to that of chapati dough.
  5. Take a small portion of the dough and flatten it into a small disc in your palm. You can use a banana leaf or a plate for this.
  6. Place a spoonful of the coconut-jaggery mixture in the center of the disc.
  7. Fold the edges over the filling and seal it, shaping it into a half-moon or any desired shape. Repeat this process for the remaining dough and filling.
  8. Line a steamer or idli maker with banana leaves to prevent sticking. If banana leaves are not available, you can use a greased plate or steaming tray.
  9. Arrange the shaped pithas in the steamer, ensuring they are not overcrowded.
  10. Steam the pithas for about 15-20 minutes or until they are cooked through and firm to the touch. You can check by inserting a toothpick; it should come out clean.
  11. Once done, remove the pithas from the steamer and let them cool slightly. Serve warm, either as a snack or dessert.
