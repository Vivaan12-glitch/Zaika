Dahi Bara

Dahi Bara is a popular Indian street food made with deep-fried lentil dumplings soaked in yogurt and topped with tamarind and mint chutneys, spices, and sev. It's a delightful snack enjoyed across the country.

Time: 1 hour

Protein:

  • Black gram (urad dal) (1 cup) provides approximately 25g of protein.

Ingredients

Steps to Make Dahi Bara:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. Soak the urad dal in water for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked dal and grind it to a smooth batter, adding a little water as needed. The batter should be thick.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Take small portions of the batter and drop them into the hot oil. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  4. In a separate pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies; saut√© for a minute.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Reduce the heat and add the yogurt. Stir continuously to avoid curdling. Cook for a few minutes until the oil separates.
  8. Add boiled and peeled potatoes, mixing them gently in the yogurt-spice mixture. Cook for a few minutes until the potatoes absorb the flavors.
  9. Gently add the fried dum baras to the gravy, ensuring they are coated well. Cook for a few more minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
