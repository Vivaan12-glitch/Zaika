Thepla

Thepla is a traditional Gujarati flatbread made with whole wheat flour, spices, and fenugreek leaves, perfect for breakfast or as a snack.

Time: 25 minutes

Protein:

  • Whole wheat flour (1 cup) provides around 13g of protein.
  • Fenugreek leaves enhance nutritional value.
  • Spices like cumin contribute small amounts of protein, though they are primarily used for flavor.

Recipe Details

Ingredients

Steps to Make Thepla:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. In a large bowl, mix 2 cups of whole wheat flour, 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of cumin powder, and 1 teaspoon of salt.
  2. Add 1 cup of finely chopped fresh fenugreek leaves (methi) and mix well.
  3. Gradually add water as needed to knead the mixture into a smooth and soft dough.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.
  5. After resting, divide the dough into equal-sized balls (about the size of a golf ball).
  6. Dust a rolling surface with flour and roll each ball into a thin circle (about 6-7 inches in diameter).
  7. Heat a tava (griddle) over medium heat.
  8. Once hot, place the rolled thepla on the tava and cook for about 30 seconds, then flip it over.
  9. Brush a little oil or ghee on the cooked side, and flip it again. Cook until both sides are golden brown and cooked through, about 1-2 minutes on each side.
  10. Remove from the tava and place it on a plate. Serve hot with yogurt, pickle, or any chutney of your choice.
