Pulihora

Pulihora, also known as tamarind rice, is a South Indian dish made with rice, tamarind, and various spices. It's a tangy and flavorful dish, perfect for any occasion.

Time: 20 minutes

Protein:

  • Peanuts (1/2 cup) provide around 14g of protein.
  • Rice (1 cup) adds about 4g of protein.

Recipe Details

Ingredients

Steps to Make Pulihora:

Listen to the instructions here:

  1. Soak 2 tablespoons of tamarind in warm water for about 15-20 minutes. Once soft, extract the juice and set it aside.
  2. Cook 2 cups of rice (preferably cooled) and let it cool completely before using.
  3. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil (preferably sesame oil) over medium heat.
  4. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then, add 1 teaspoon of urad dal and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add 2-3 broken dry red chilies, 2 slit green chilies, and a handful of curry leaves. Sauté until fragrant.
  6. Stir in the extracted tamarind juice, salt to taste, and cook for a few minutes until the mixture thickens slightly.
  7. Add the cooked rice to the tamarind mixture and gently mix until the rice is well coated.
  8. If desired, stir in 2 tablespoons of roasted peanuts and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  9. Serve warm with papad or pickle.
